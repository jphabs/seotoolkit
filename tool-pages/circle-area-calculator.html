The area of a circle is an important mathematical measurement, and it is calculated using the formula:

Area = π * r²

Where π (pi) is approximately 3.14159, and r is the radius of the circle.

This formula gives you the total space inside the circle.

The larger the radius, the greater the area.

Another crucial aspect of a circle is its circumference, which represents the distance around the edge.

The circumference can be calculated using the formula:

Circumference = 2 * π * r

By inputting the radius into the calculator, you can also find the circumference, which is particularly useful in a wide range of applications from construction projects to design work.

Lastly, the diameter is the straight-line distance across the circle, passing through its center.

You can easily find the diameter by simply doubling the radius:

Diameter = 2 * r

This measurement is essential when you need to understand the full width of the circle.
